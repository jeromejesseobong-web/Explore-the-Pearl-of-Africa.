Wildlife Conservation in Uganda

Tourism supports the protection of wildlife, ecosystems, and local communities.

Protecting the Pearl of Africa

Uganda is home to diverse ecosystems including rainforests, savannahs, wetlands, and mountains. Sustainable tourism plays a vital role in conserving wildlife and supporting local communities. Tourism revenue helps fund national parks, anti-poaching patrols, wildlife monitoring, and environmental education programs.

Our Conservation Efforts

Gorilla Protection

Uganda protects nearly half of the world’s remaining mountain gorillas through conservation programs in Bwindi Impenetrable National Park.

  • Habitat protection
  • Veterinary care
  • Gorilla population monitoring

Anti-Poaching Patrols

Wildlife rangers conduct daily patrols to prevent illegal hunting and protect endangered species across Uganda’s national parks.

  • Ranger patrol teams
  • Wildlife protection units
  • Law enforcement support

Community Based Tourism

Local communities benefit from tourism through employment, cultural tourism activities, and conservation education programs.

  • Local guide employment
  • Cultural tourism experiences
  • Community development projects
